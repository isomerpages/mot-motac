        <div class="col-sm-6">
          <p> Compared to reading a blog, <span class="font-weight-bold">four in five people today are more likely to watch a video</span>. This increase in consumption of videos has led to <span class="font-weight-bold">livestreaming</span> features from Facebook and other platforms. </p>
          <p> Livestreaming is commonly used to create a virtual shopping experience for online customers. However, for LTA, we <span class="font-weight-bold">capitalised on this growing trend</span> to use live streaming as our digital strategy to reach out to build relationship and connection with the public, <span class="font-weight-bold"> positively shaping their perception</span> of land transport. </p>
          <p class="text-decoration-underline font-weight-bold"> Arrival of New Trains: An Opportunity Not To Be Missed </p>
          <p> The public have always looked forward to our new rail assets such as the latest trains that hail from all over the world to add/replace our fleets. </p>
          <p> Such transport updates typically garner extensive media coverage on most, if not all, mainstream and online news platforms. They also elicit <span class="font-weight-bold">high levels of online chatter, </span> reflecting how excited netizens are when it comes to such news. </p>
          <p> We thus saw the arrivals of our new/refurbished trains as an opportunity to take viewers behind-the-scenes through <span class="font-weight-bold"> Facebook Livestreaming for greater reach, exposure, and engagement with netizens.</span>
          </p>
